# Top 50 English Grammar Rules

> Reference for FluentAI Coach — used to identify and explain grammar errors during speech analysis.

---

## 1. Subject-Verb Agreement

The verb must agree with its subject in number.

- ✅ "She **runs** every morning."
- ❌ "She **run** every morning."
- Rule: Singular subjects take singular verbs; plural subjects take plural verbs.

## 2. Tense Consistency

Don't shift tenses within a sentence or paragraph without reason.

- ✅ "I **walked** to the store and **bought** milk."
- ❌ "I **walked** to the store and **buy** milk."

## 3. Articles (a, an, the)

Use "a" before consonant sounds, "an" before vowel sounds, "the" for specific references.

- ✅ "I saw **an** elephant at **the** zoo."
- ❌ "I saw **a** elephant at **a** zoo." (when referring to a specific zoo)

## 4. Present Perfect vs. Simple Past

Use present perfect for actions with present relevance; simple past for completed actions.

- ✅ "I **have lived** here for 5 years." (still living here)
- ✅ "I **lived** there for 5 years." (no longer living there)
- ❌ "I **live** here for 5 years."

## 5. Pronoun-Antecedent Agreement

Pronouns must agree with the nouns they replace.

- ✅ "Each student should bring **their** laptop." (accepted singular they)
- ❌ "The team lost **his** game."

## 6. Dangling Modifiers

Modifiers must clearly relate to the word they modify.

- ✅ "**Running quickly**, **I** caught the bus."
- ❌ "**Running quickly**, **the bus** was caught." (the bus wasn't running)

## 7. Comma Splices

Don't join two independent clauses with just a comma.

- ✅ "I like coffee**;** she likes tea."
- ✅ "I like coffee**, and** she likes tea."
- ❌ "I like coffee**,** she likes tea."

## 8. Its vs. It's

"It's" = it is / it has. "Its" = possessive.

- ✅ "**It's** raining." / "The cat licked **its** paw."
- ❌ "**Its** raining." / "The cat licked **it's** paw."

## 9. Who vs. Whom

"Who" = subject. "Whom" = object.

- ✅ "**Who** is calling?" / "To **whom** should I speak?"
- Tip: If you can replace with "he/she," use "who." If "him/her," use "whom."

## 10. Fewer vs. Less

"Fewer" for countable nouns. "Less" for uncountable.

- ✅ "**Fewer** people attended." / "**Less** water was used."
- ❌ "**Less** people attended."

## 11. Double Negatives

Avoid using two negatives in one clause.

- ✅ "I **don't** have **any** money."
- ❌ "I **don't** have **no** money."

## 12. Preposition at End of Sentence

Acceptable in modern English, but formal writing sometimes avoids it.

- ✅ "What are you looking **for**?" (natural)
- ✅ "**For** what are you looking?" (formal)

## 13. Parallel Structure

Items in a list or comparison should follow the same grammatical form.

- ✅ "She likes **swimming**, **running**, and **cycling**."
- ❌ "She likes **swimming**, **to run**, and **cycling**."

## 14. Conditional Sentences (Type 1)

Real/possible conditions: If + present simple, will + base verb.

- ✅ "If it **rains**, I **will** stay home."
- ❌ "If it **will rain**, I **will** stay home."

## 15. Conditional Sentences (Type 2)

Unreal/hypothetical: If + past simple, would + base verb.

- ✅ "If I **had** more time, I **would** travel."
- ❌ "If I **have** more time, I **would** travel."

## 16. Conditional Sentences (Type 3)

Past unreal: If + past perfect, would have + past participle.

- ✅ "If I **had studied**, I **would have passed**."
- ❌ "If I **would have studied**, I **would have passed**."

## 17. Reported Speech

Shift tense back when reporting.

- Direct: "I **am** tired."
- Reported: She said she **was** tired.
- ❌ She said she **is** tired. (unless still true at time of reporting)

## 18. Gerund vs. Infinitive

Some verbs take gerunds (-ing), some take infinitives (to + verb).

- ✅ "I enjoy **swimming**." / "I want **to swim**."
- ❌ "I enjoy **to swim**." / "I want **swimming**."
- Common gerund verbs: enjoy, avoid, consider, finish, mind, suggest
- Common infinitive verbs: want, need, decide, plan, hope, expect

## 19. Comparative and Superlative Forms

One syllable: -er/-est. Two+: more/most.

- ✅ "**bigger**, **biggest**" / "**more beautiful**, **most beautiful**"
- ❌ "**more big**" / "**beautifuller**"

## 20. Apostrophes for Possession

Singular: 's. Plural ending in s: s'.

- ✅ "The **dog's** bone." / "The **dogs'** bones."
- ❌ "The **dogs** bone." (missing apostrophe)

## 21. There/Their/They're

- "There" = location. "Their" = possession. "They're" = they are.
- ✅ "**They're** putting **their** bags over **there**."

## 22. Your vs. You're

- "Your" = possession. "You're" = you are.
- ✅ "**You're** holding **your** phone."

## 23. Affect vs. Effect

- "Affect" = verb (to influence). "Effect" = noun (result).
- ✅ "The weather **affects** my mood." / "The **effect** was immediate."

## 24. Run-on Sentences

Don't fuse independent clauses without punctuation.

- ✅ "I finished the book. It was great."
- ❌ "I finished the book it was great."

## 25. Sentence Fragments

Every sentence needs a subject and a verb.

- ✅ "She **went** to the store."
- ❌ "Went to the store." (no subject)

## 26. Could/Would/Should + Have

Never "could of" — it's "could have" (could've).

- ✅ "I **could have** helped."
- ❌ "I **could of** helped."

## 27. Much vs. Many

"Much" for uncountable. "Many" for countable.

- ✅ "How **much** time?" / "How **many** days?"
- ❌ "How **much** days?"

## 28. Adjective Order

Opinion → Size → Age → Shape → Color → Origin → Material → Purpose.

- ✅ "A **beautiful large old** house."
- ❌ "A **old large beautiful** house."

## 29. Passive Voice Construction

Be + past participle.

- ✅ "The letter **was written** by John."
- ❌ "The letter **was wrote** by John."

## 30. Since vs. For (Duration)

"Since" = point in time. "For" = period of time.

- ✅ "I've worked here **since** 2020." / "I've worked here **for** 4 years."
- ❌ "I've worked here **since** 4 years."

## 31. Used to / Be Used to / Get Used to

- "Used to" = past habit (I used to swim).
- "Be used to" = accustomed (I'm used to swimming).
- "Get used to" = becoming accustomed (I'm getting used to swimming).

## 32. Relative Clauses (Who/Which/That)

- "Who" for people. "Which" for things. "That" for both (restrictive).
- ✅ "The man **who** called." / "The book **that/which** I read."

## 33. Neither...Nor / Either...Or

Paired correctly with matching structure.

- ✅ "**Neither** John **nor** Mary is coming."
- ❌ "**Neither** John **or** Mary is coming."

## 34. Wish + Past Tense (Unreal Present)

- ✅ "I wish I **had** more time." (I don't have enough time now)
- ❌ "I wish I **have** more time."

## 35. Lay vs. Lie

- "Lay" = transitive (lay something down). "Lie" = intransitive (lie down).
- ✅ "I'll **lie** down." / "**Lay** the book on the table."

## 36. Among vs. Between

- "Between" for two. "Among" for three or more.
- ✅ "**Between** you and me." / "**Among** all the students."

## 37. Subjunctive Mood

Use base form after verbs of suggestion/demand.

- ✅ "I suggest he **be** on time."
- ❌ "I suggest he **is** on time."

## 38. Collective Nouns

Can be singular or plural depending on context.

- ✅ "The team **is** winning." (as a unit)
- ✅ "The team **are** arguing." (as individuals, British English)

## 39. Adverb Placement

Adverbs of frequency go before the main verb (after "be").

- ✅ "She **always** arrives early." / "She is **always** early."
- ❌ "She arrives **always** early."

## 40. So vs. Such

"So" + adjective. "Such" + noun phrase.

- ✅ "It was **so** beautiful." / "It was **such** a beautiful day."
- ❌ "It was **such** beautiful."

## 41. Despite / In spite of + Noun/-ing

- ✅ "**Despite** the rain, we went out."
- ❌ "**Despite of** the rain, we went out."

## 42. Like vs. As

"Like" + noun/pronoun. "As" + clause.

- ✅ "She sings **like** an angel." / "Do **as** I say."
- ❌ "Do **like** I say."

## 43. Singular They

Accepted for unknown or non-binary gender.

- ✅ "Someone left **their** umbrella."

## 44. Verb Patterns with Make/Let/Help

"Make" and "let" + bare infinitive. "Help" + bare infinitive or to-infinitive.

- ✅ "She **made** me **laugh**." / "Let me **go**."
- ❌ "She **made** me **to laugh**."

## 45. Question Tag Formation

Positive statement → negative tag (and vice versa).

- ✅ "You're coming, **aren't you**?"
- ❌ "You're coming, **are you**?"

## 46. Still / Yet / Already

- "Still" = continuing. "Yet" = not happened (questions/negatives). "Already" = sooner than expected.
- ✅ "I'm **still** waiting." / "Have you finished **yet**?" / "I've **already** eaten."

## 47. Say vs. Tell

"Say" + (to someone). "Tell" + someone directly.

- ✅ "She **said** it was fine." / "She **told** me it was fine."
- ❌ "She **said** me it was fine."

## 48. Unless = If...not

- ✅ "**Unless** you hurry, you'll be late." = "**If** you **don't** hurry, you'll be late."
- ❌ "**Unless** you **don't** hurry..." (double negative)

## 49. Enough + Noun / Adjective + Enough

- ✅ "**Enough** time." / "Good **enough**."
- ❌ "**Enough** good."

## 50. Too vs. Very

"Too" implies excess (negative). "Very" is just emphasis.

- ✅ "It's **too** hot to go outside." (excess — can't go)
- ✅ "It's **very** hot today." (emphasis — just describing)
- ❌ "It's **too** hot today." (when you just mean very)
