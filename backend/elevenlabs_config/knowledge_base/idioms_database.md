## Business & Work (25)

1. **Break the ice** — Start a conversation in an awkward situation. *"Let me break the ice — I'm Sarah from marketing."*
2. **Get the ball rolling** — Start a process or activity. *"Let's get the ball rolling on this project."*
3. **Think outside the box** — Think creatively. *"We need to think outside the box to solve this."*
4. **Back to the drawing board** — Start over from scratch. *"The client rejected it — back to the drawing board."*
5. **Cut corners** — Do something cheaply or badly. *"Don't cut corners on quality."*
6. **In the loop** — Informed and up to date. *"Keep me in the loop on the progress."*
7. **On the same page** — In agreement. *"Let's make sure we're on the same page."*
8. **Raise the bar** — Set higher standards. *"This product really raises the bar."*
9. **Touch base** — Check in briefly. *"Let's touch base tomorrow morning."*
10. **Up in the air** — Uncertain, undecided. *"The timeline is still up in the air."*
11. **Wear many hats** — Have multiple roles. *"In a startup, you wear many hats."*
12. **The bottom line** — The most important point. *"The bottom line is we need more revenue."*
13. **Go the extra mile** — Do more than expected. *"She always goes the extra mile for clients."*
14. **Hit the ground running** — Start working effectively immediately. *"The new hire hit the ground running."*
15. **Learn the ropes** — Learn the basics of a job. *"It took a month to learn the ropes."*
16. **Call the shots** — Make the important decisions. *"She calls the shots in this department."*
17. **Burning the midnight oil** — Working very late. *"We've been burning the midnight oil to meet the deadline."*
18. **A tough nut to crack** — A difficult problem or person. *"That client is a tough nut to crack."*
19. **Ahead of the curve** — More advanced than others. *"Their technology is ahead of the curve."*
20. **Bring to the table** — Offer or contribute. *"What skills do you bring to the table?"*
21. **Drop the ball** — Make a mistake, fail to follow through. *"Someone dropped the ball on this order."*
22. **From the ground up** — From the very beginning. *"We built this company from the ground up."*
23. **Move the needle** — Make a noticeable difference. *"Will this campaign move the needle?"*
24. **Put all your eggs in one basket** — Risk everything on one thing. *"Don't put all your eggs in one basket."*
25. **Win-win situation** — Beneficial for all parties. *"This deal is a win-win situation."*
