## Business & Work (25)

1. **Break the ice** — Start a conversation in an awkward situation. _"Let me break the ice — I'm Sarah from marketing."_
2. **Get the ball rolling** — Start a process or activity. _"Let's get the ball rolling on this project."_
3. **Think outside the box** — Think creatively. _"We need to think outside the box to solve this."_
4. **Back to the drawing board** — Start over from scratch. _"The client rejected it — back to the drawing board."_
5. **Cut corners** — Do something cheaply or badly. _"Don't cut corners on quality."_
6. **In the loop** — Informed and up to date. _"Keep me in the loop on the progress."_
7. **On the same page** — In agreement. _"Let's make sure we're on the same page."_
8. **Raise the bar** — Set higher standards. _"This product really raises the bar."_
9. **Touch base** — Check in briefly. _"Let's touch base tomorrow morning."_
10. **Up in the air** — Uncertain, undecided. _"The timeline is still up in the air."_
11. **Wear many hats** — Have multiple roles. _"In a startup, you wear many hats."_
12. **The bottom line** — The most important point. _"The bottom line is we need more revenue."_
13. **Go the extra mile** — Do more than expected. _"She always goes the extra mile for clients."_
14. **Hit the ground running** — Start working effectively immediately. _"The new hire hit the ground running."_
15. **Learn the ropes** — Learn the basics of a job. _"It took a month to learn the ropes."_
16. **Call the shots** — Make the important decisions. _"She calls the shots in this department."_
17. **Burning the midnight oil** — Working very late. _"We've been burning the midnight oil to meet the deadline."_
18. **A tough nut to crack** — A difficult problem or person. _"That client is a tough nut to crack."_
19. **Ahead of the curve** — More advanced than others. _"Their technology is ahead of the curve."_
20. **Bring to the table** — Offer or contribute. _"What skills do you bring to the table?"_
21. **Drop the ball** — Make a mistake, fail to follow through. _"Someone dropped the ball on this order."_
22. **From the ground up** — From the very beginning. _"We built this company from the ground up."_
23. **Move the needle** — Make a noticeable difference. _"Will this campaign move the needle?"_
24. **Put all your eggs in one basket** — Risk everything on one thing. _"Don't put all your eggs in one basket."_
25. **Win-win situation** — Beneficial for all parties. _"This deal is a win-win situation."_

## Time & Urgency (20)

26. **Better late than never** — It's better to do something late than not at all.

28. **Time flies** — Time passes quickly. _"Time flies when you're having fun."_

35. **Take your time** — Don't rush. _"Take your time — there's no hurry."_
36. **Beat the clock** — Finish before a deadline. _"We beat the clock by 5 minutes."_
37. **Day in, day out** — Every day, repeatedly. _"He practices day in, day out."_
38. **Behind the times** — Old-fashioned. _"Their website is behind the times."_
39. **Ahead of time** — Before the scheduled time. _"She always arrives ahead of time."_
40. **Stand the test of time** — Remain good over time. _"Great design stands the test of time."_
41. **In the long run** — Over a long period. _"It'll save money in the long run."_
42. **At this point in time** — Now, currently. _"At this point in time, we have no updates."_
43. **A race against time** — Urgent deadline. _"The rescue was a race against time."_
44. **Time is money** — Don't waste time. _"Let's not waste it — time is money."_
45. **From time to time** — Occasionally. _"I visit from time to time."_
